AMERICAN FORK, UTAH, November 1, 2011 — Computational Simulation Software, LLC (csimsoft) is pleased to announce that Paul Ressler has joined the company as the director of sales and marketing. Ressler combines his experience with developing successful marketing strategies with past experience as a business owner to position csimsoft for continued future growth.

"csimsoft is experiencing tremendous growth," said Randy Morris, csimsoft's managing director. "Mr. Ressler's marketing expertise and strategic vision will help solidify csimsoft's market position and will provide the marketing and sales support that will ensure our continued growth."

Ressler has over 19 years of experience working for both large companies and small start-ups. He has worked for software companies, including a division of Lexis-Nexis that provides document assembly and electronic publishing solutions for large companies, government agencies, and law firms. He consulted with these large organizations to design sophisticated networking and custom workflow solutions. Ressler has experience developing strategic partner programs and managing these relationships. He co-founded two successful companies where he oversaw business structure, finance, and new business development. Ressler was the president of Hybrid Dynamics Corporation (OTBB:HBDY). In these positions he oversees all aspects of the public company, including corporate structuring, SEC compliance and reporting, investor relations, raising capital, and strategic direction. Ressler also served on the Board of Trustees of the Top Flight Long-Short Fund (TOPFX), an open-end, no-load mutual fund with over $17 million in assets.

"csimsoft has a strong reputation for producing excellent tools for finite element analysis," said Ressler. "And, it is quickly becoming the pre-processor of choice for generating high-quality meshes for CFD." csimsoft's growth over the past few years has sprung from the use of its products by government contractors, academic, and research entities associated with Sandia National Laboratories. In 2004, csimsoft was formed to address the needs of non-U.S. government users. Its user-base now extends worldwide.

About csimsoft

csimsoft is a leading developer of pre- and post-processing solutions for the computational fluid dynamics and finite element analysis communities. csimsoft was founded in 2004 and is headquartered in American Fork, Utah.
